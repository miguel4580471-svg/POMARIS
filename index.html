The company

POMARIS S.A.C. is a Peruvian agro-export company specialized in the production, packing, and international marketing of fresh Wonderful variety pomegranates (HS heading 0810.90). Based in Ica, with a harvest season from March to July, it supplies the U.S. market precisely when supply from the Northern Hemisphere decreases.

Mission & Vision

Mission

To produce, process, and export premium-quality fresh Wonderful variety pomegranates, leveraging the agroclimatic conditions of Ica and a rigorous cold chain, in order to meet U.S. market demand with fresh, healthy, and reliable fruit during the Peruvian seasonal window.

Vision

To be recognized by 2030 as a leading and reliable Peruvian supplier of fresh pomegranates in the international market, standing out for operational excellence, sustainability, and long-term commercial partnerships with top-tier distributors.

SWOT Analysis

Strengths

  • Strategic commercial window (March–July), outside the Northern Hemisphere season.
  • Located in Ica, a leading region with optimal agroclimatic conditions.
  • Modern irrigation and post-harvest infrastructure.
  • Controlled cold chain (5–8 °C) with a 6 to 8-week shelf life.
  • Hand harvesting that preserves fruit quality and crown.
  • SENASA phytosanitary and GlobalG.A.P. certifications.

Opportunities

  • The U.S. is the best-rated market (score 4.00) for size and potential.
  • Peru–U.S. Free Trade Agreement with 0% tariff for HS heading 0810.90.
  • Strong growth of Peruvian exports to the U.S. (39.6% between 2024–2025).
  • Peru is the third-largest pomegranate supplier to the U.S. market.
  • Growing demand driven by the product’s health benefits.

Weaknesses

  • Peru ranks 12th globally in volume under HS heading 0810.90.
  • Highly perishable product, sensitive during logistics.
  • Dependence on a short seasonal window.
  • Smaller scale compared to structural global competitors.

Threats

  • Mexico as the main competitor in the U.S. (proximity and lower logistics costs).
  • Ecuador as direct regional competition.
  • Turkey as a strong structural competitor worldwide.
  • Cold-chain risks during the 20–21 day ocean transit.
  • Phytosanitary barriers and control of maximum residue limits.
